Dr. Wangari Muta Maathai

A life well lived

It is often difficult to describe to those who live in a free society what life is like in an authoritarian regime. You don't know who to trust. You worry that you, your family, or your friends will be arrested and jailed without due process. The fear of political violence or death, whether through direct assassinations or targeted "accidents", is constant. Such was the case in Kenya, especially during the 1990s.


Wangari Muta Maathai – Unbowed, p. 206.

Wangari Muta Maathai (1 April 1940 – 25 September 2011) was a Kenyan environmental and political activist. She was educated in the United States at Mount St. Scholastica (Benedictine College) and the University of Pittsburgh, as well as the University of Nairobi in Kenya.

Some highlights of her life:

  • 1 April 1940 born in the village of Ihithe, Nyeri District, in the central highlands of Kenya.
  • 1964 Bachelor of Science Degree in Biology with with minors in chemistry and German - Mount St. Scholastica College (now Benedictine College) Atchison, Kansas USA
  • 1966 Master of Science Degree in Biology - University of Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh, USA
  • 1969 Marries Mwangi Mathai
  • 1970 son Waweru is born
  • 1971 PhD in veterinary anatomy - University of Nairobi, Nairobi, Kenya, becoming first Eastern African woman to receive a PhD
  • December 1971 daughter Wanjira is born
  • 1974 third child Muta is born
  • 1977 founded the Green Belt Movement, an environmental non-governmental organization focused on the planting of trees, environmental conservation, and women's rights.
  • 1984 awarded the Right Livelihood Award
  • 2004 First African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize for "her contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace".
  • Elected member of Parliament
  • Assistant minister for Environment and Natural Resources in the government of President Mwai Kibaki between January 2003 and November 2005.
  • Honorary Councillor of the World Future Council.
  • 2011 Maathai died of complications from ovarian cancer.
